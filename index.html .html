Our Framework

What We Can & Cannot Do —
Stated Plainly

A transparent breakdown of how we operate within the legal framework governing advocate marketing in India.

✓  What We Can Help With

✓

BCI-Approved Website Information

Name, enrolment number, address, contact details, qualifications, bar association memberships, and areas of practice — exactly as permitted by the 2008 amendment to Rule 36.

✓

Educational Legal Content

Legal explainers, articles, and guides published under the advocate's name. Educational content is widely considered distinct from solicitation and generally permissible.

✓

Professional Profile Management

LinkedIn profiles, Google Business Profiles, and directory listings containing only factual, BCI-permitted information about the advocate's practice and qualifications.

✓

Informational Discoverability

Helping advocates appear in search results for informational queries so potential clients can find accurate contact and qualification information.

✗  What We Do Not Do (BCI Prohibited)

✗

No Paid Advertising (PPC / Google Ads)

Running paid search ads to acquire legal clients constitutes direct solicitation, which is prohibited under Rule 36. We do not run Google Ads or Meta Ads targeting legal clients for advocates.

✗

No Lead Generation Campaigns

Platforms that match clients to lawyers have been subject to BCI cease-and-desist notices. We do not operate lead generation services or client-matching platforms.

✗

No Celebrity or Influencer Promotions

The BCI March 2025 directive explicitly prohibits use of celebrities or social media influencers for legal promotions. We do not engage in any such promotional activities.

✗

No Misleading Claims or Guarantees

We never create content making guarantees about legal outcomes, claiming superiority over other advocates, or using misleading statements about an advocate's practice.
